# Chapter 2
## The First Tuesday

Tuesday nights always belonged to the lathe.

By eleven, the streets outside had gone quiet, but inside the workshop the machine never rested. Sparks flew across the room as steel met steel. The smell of hot metal filled the air.

Vivin liked working alone.

There were no customers asking questions. No phones ringing. Just him, the machine, and the steady rhythm he had lived with for more than twenty years.

He checked the wall clock.

**11:47 PM.**

Another ordinary Tuesday.

He picked up the next steel rod and tightened it into the chuck. The machine roared back to life.

Then...

Something felt different.

Not wrong.

Just... different.

The machine suddenly sounded distant, as if someone had closed a door between him and the world.

Vivin stopped what he was doing.

The workshop was silent.

Too silent.

He turned around.

Nobody.

The front shutter was still locked.

Murugan had gone home hours ago.

He laughed to himself.

"You're getting old, Vivin."

He switched the machine back on.

The sound returned.

For a few seconds, everything was normal again.

Then the lights flickered.

Once.

Twice.

The machine slowed without losing power.

The air around him felt strangely cold.

Before he could understand what was happening—

Everything disappeared.

---

The noise was gone.

The workshop was gone.

Warm sand pressed against his feet.

A cool breeze touched his face.

The smell of salt filled the air.

He stood on a quiet beach that stretched farther than he could see.

The sea moved gently, as though it had nowhere to be.

A small boy stood a few steps away.

He looked no older than eight.

He wasn't crying.

He wasn't smiling.

He simply looked confused.

The two of them stared at each other.

Finally, the boy spoke.

"...You can see me too?"

Vivin frowned.

"What?"

The boy slowly took a step closer.

"You can actually see me?"

Before Vivin could answer—

The beach vanished.

---

The sound of the lathe crashed back into his ears.

The steel rod was still spinning.

Sparks flew exactly as before.

He grabbed the machine and switched it off.

His heart pounded.

His breathing became heavy.

He looked around the workshop.

Everything was exactly where it should be.

The toolbox.

The oil can.

Murugan's gloves hanging on the wall.

Nothing had changed.

He looked at the clock.

**11:47 PM.**

Only two seconds had passed.

"...No."

He checked again.

The second hand continued moving as if nothing had happened.

He walked to the wash basin and splashed water on his face.

Cold.

Real.

He touched the wall.

Solid.

He looked at his hands.

They trembled slightly.

"What was that...?"

No answer came.

---

He closed the workshop earlier than usual.

The ride home felt longer that night.

The streets were almost empty.

A few street dogs lifted their heads as he passed, then settled back to sleep.

When he reached home, the kitchen light was still on.

His wife looked up from the dining table.

"You're early."

"Hmm."

She placed a plate in front of him.

"You look tired."

"Just work."

She watched him for a moment.

"You've been doing too many night shifts."

He forced a smile.

"I'll be fine."

She didn't argue.

But she knew something wasn't right.

---

The week moved on.

On Wednesday, Vivin almost gave the wrong part to a customer.

On Thursday, he stood in front of the lathe for several minutes before realizing the machine wasn't even switched on.

On Friday, he caught himself staring at a beach wallpaper on someone's phone until they walked away.

On Saturday, Murugan asked,

"Anna... are you okay?"

Vivin almost told him.

Almost.

Instead, he smiled.

"Didn't sleep well."

On Sunday, he visited the temple.

He stood there for a long time.

He prayed.

Not for money.

Not for health.

Just for peace in his mind.

By Monday night...

Sleep had become impossible.

Every time he closed his eyes, he heard waves.

Every time he opened them...

He was back in his room.

---

Tuesday arrived again.

Vivin unlocked the workshop.

The familiar smell of oil and steel greeted him.

He stood at the entrance for a moment.

Nothing.

No strange feeling.

No beach.

No child.

He shook his head and smiled.

"Maybe I really am getting old."

He switched on the lathe.

The machine began to spin.

Then...

Something appeared in front of him.

A tiny circle.

No bigger than a coin.

It floated silently in the air.

Vivin stared.

The circle slowly grew.

He took one careful step forward.

A child's voice came from the other side.

"You came back."
