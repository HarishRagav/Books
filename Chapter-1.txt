

THE LAST TUESDAY

Chapter 1 – The Man Everyone Knew

The sound of metal never stopped near Tirunelveli Junction.

From early morning until late at night, sparks flew from a small lathe shop at the corner of an old street. The board above the entrance was faded by the sun.

**Vivin Engineering Works.**

People rarely looked at the board anymore.

They already knew the man inside.

"Morning, Vivin anna!"

A milkman slowed his bicycle just enough to wave.

Vivin looked up from the machine and smiled.

"Morning!"

The machine screamed again as steel touched steel. Tiny sparks danced through the air before disappearing on the concrete floor.

The smell of hot iron had become part of his life.

He no longer noticed it.

---

At exactly eight o'clock, an old man walked into the shop carrying a broken water pump.

"Can you fix it?"

Vivin turned the pump in his hands.

"When do you need it?"

"Today."

Vivin smiled.

"Then today it is."

The old man nodded with relief.

"I knew you'd say that."

---

Around noon, the sun turned the streets white with heat.

A thin young man stood quietly outside the shop.

His shirt was old.

His slippers were worn out.

He looked at the machines with both fear and hope.

Vivin noticed him after a few minutes.

"You've been standing there for a long time."

The young man smiled nervously.

"I... I'm looking for work."

"What work do you know?"

"I'll learn anything."

Vivin looked at him for a few seconds.

"What is your name?"

"Murugan."

"Can you be honest?"

Murugan blinked.

"I'll try."

Vivin laughed.

"No."

Murugan looked confused.

"You don't try to be honest."

Vivin held out his hand.

"You either are...

...or you aren't."

Murugan slowly shook his hand.

"When can I start?"

Vivin picked up a pair of safety gloves from a shelf and threw them toward him.

"They're yours now."

Murugan caught them with both hands.

"Now."

---

The first few weeks were difficult.

Murugan made mistakes.

Lots of them.

Wrong measurements.

Bent rods.

Broken drill bits.

Sometimes he stood silently, waiting to be shouted at.

The shouting never came.

Instead...

Vivin would stand beside him.

"Come."

He would take the tool from Murugan's hand.

"Look at the machine."

Not the tool.

"The machine tells you what it wants."

Murugan frowned.

"It's just iron."

Vivin smiled.

"No."

He gently touched the metal.

"It talks."

Murugan laughed.

"I think you've worked too much."

"Maybe."

Vivin laughed with him.

"But listen anyway."

Months passed.

The mistakes became fewer.

The confidence became stronger.

One evening, Murugan finished a difficult job all by himself.

He looked at Vivin like a child waiting for approval.

Vivin checked every measurement.

Then quietly nodded.

"Perfect."

Murugan smiled all the way home.

---

By the end of the year, the shop had more work than ever.

Machines ran all day.

Customers came from nearby towns.

Sometimes even from villages nearly fifty kilometers away.

One evening, Murugan looked around at the pile of unfinished work.

"Anna..."

"We can't finish all this."

Vivin wiped the sweat from his forehead.

"We can."

"How?"

"You take mornings."

Murugan looked surprised.

"And you?"

"I like nights."

"You'll never sleep."

Vivin grinned.

"I never liked sleeping much."

From that day...

The shop never closed.

When Murugan locked it in the evening...

Vivin opened it again an hour later.

People joked that the machines themselves had forgotten how to rest.

---

Every night, just before starting work, Vivin opened the small fridge in the corner.

Inside...

There was always one bottle.

Cold.

Covered with tiny drops of water.

Appy Fizz.

He opened it.

*Tsshhh...*

The sound made him smile every single time.

He took one slow sip.

Closed his eyes.

"Perfect."

Murugan laughed the first time he saw it.

"Anna... after all these years..."

"Still Appy Fizz?"

Vivin looked offended.

"What do you mean 'still'?"

"I thought one day you'd become a tea or coffee man."

Vivin shook his head.

"No chance."

Murugan laughed.

"I'll remember that."

Vivin raised the bottle.

"You better."

---

Outside...

A brown street dog sat near the entrance.

It wasn't hungry.

It wasn't barking.

It was waiting.

Vivin reached into his bag.

Without looking...

He threw a biscuit.

The dog caught it before it touched the ground.

Another dog appeared.

Then another.

Within seconds...

Five wagging tails surrounded him.

Murugan stared.

"They know exactly when you're leaving."

"They're smarter than us."

"Why?"

"They never forget kindness."

Murugan looked at the dogs.

Then quietly looked at Vivin.

Maybe...

Neither did people.
